Risk of Acute Exacerbations

Sometimes, IPF can worsen because of an acute exacerbation. An acute exacerbation is a sudden and unpredictable worsening of IPF that often results in hospitalization and can even be fatal.


During an acute exacerbation, you could experience:

Breathing becoming more difficult than usual

Worsening cough

Fever or flu-like symptoms

If you experience the symptoms above, be sure to call your doctor right away.


An acute exacerbation can occur at any time during the course of the disease—from diagnosis of IPF to more advanced IPF.


People who have an acute exacerbation have a higher risk of another acute exacerbation. A person’s condition after an acute exacerbation can vary, but it often results in shortened survival due to the exacerbation’s new lung damage that worsens FVC and DLCO.
