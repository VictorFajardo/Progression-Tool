What Is IPF Progression?

Before learning about how IPF progresses, let’s start by defining what progression is. Progression means that IPF will continue to worsen over time and cause permanent and irreversible damage to the lungs. This progression can present itself many different ways.


Some people may notice worsening symptoms, see changes in tests , like high-resolution computed tomography (HRCT) scans or diffusing capacity (DLCO), or have to go on supplemental oxygen, while others may not notice any signs of progression for a long time.

HELPFUL HINT

Tests used to detect IPF progression include:

  • HRCT scans
  • Pulse oximetry (measured at rest and with exertion)
  • Exercise/walking tests
  • Spirometry (forced vital capacity [FVC])
  • DLCO
  • Lung volume measurement (total lung capacity [TLC])
  • Arterial blood gas testing
  • Questionnaire, like the University of California San Diego Shortness of Breath Questionnaire

No matter how IPF affects you, it’s important to understand that the disease is always progressing at varying rates, which is why it’s so important to take action early.
