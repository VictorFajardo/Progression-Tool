The Lungs’ Healing Process

Sometimes the lungs are exposed to harmful events that cause injuries to the tiny air sacs inside the lungs, called alveoli. The body responds by sending a variety of cells to heal the injury, and one type is called fibroblasts.

In a normal lung, the healing process ends once the lung has returned to a healthy state.

In the lung of a person with IPF, the healing process does not end.

It is thought that fibroblasts do not “turn off” and, instead, continuously try to heal the lungs, leading to a buildup of scar tissue.
